Foundations

Principles

Six rules that decide every call. When in doubt, remove something.

01
Less, but enough

Always reach for removal before addition. This tool exists because Jira and Linear got noisy — every badge, line, and toolbar must earn its place.

02
One person

There is never a second user. No avatars, assignees, mentions, or “assigned to me.” Everything belongs to one developer.

03
Two independent axes

Module (sub-system) and Milestone (phase) must read as clearly different things — never conflated, never visually similar.

04
Status note ≠ comments

The pinned note (“what’s blocking me now”) is elevated and always visible. Comments are a quiet append-only log. They never look alike.

05
Capture first, sort later

Quick-add and Inbox remove friction from recording an idea. Triage is a separate, unhurried step.

06
Hover-reveal

Rows are calm at rest and disclose secondary metadata and actions on hover. Primary info stays loud; the rest waits.

Color

Everything is a token. Neutrals carry a faint warm tint (hue 95); the accent is the only saturated color in the chrome. Defining colors as variables means dark mode swaps cleanly with zero hardcoded values.

Radius & Density

Corners are soft but restrained. The whole radius scale is multiplied by --radius-scale and row padding by density knobs, so the entire UI can soften or tighten from one variable.

Radii
Tweakable knobs. --radius-scale (0.5–1.6), --shadow-strength (0–1.6), --row-pad-y, and --ui-scale let a user reshape density and softness live without touching component CSS.
Foundations

Elevation

Shadows are whisper-soft and warm-tinted — elevation you feel before you see. Four levels, from a hairline lift to the slide-in detail panel.

Motion
